The Sustainable Lifestyle Store

What are you selling?

Our store offers a variety of sustainable and eco-friendly products aimed at reducing environmental impact. These include reusable items such as water bottles, shopping bags, and food containers, as well as energy-efficient appliances, organic personal care products, and eco-friendly cleaning supplies. We also provide tools to help users make their life more sustainable, such as the "Carbon Footprint Calculator" and the "Water Usage Calculator."

What additional page(s) did you add? How to operate it?

  • User Settings: You can change your personal details, including your sustainable category and password (communicated with the server). Just click on the account icon and fill the form.
  • Orders: Each user can see their recent orders (communicated with the server), accessible through the account icon menu.
  • Your Reviews: After ordering and paying for a product, users can leave a review that will appear on the reviews page. This is also accessed through the account icon menu.
  • Activities: Each user can see their activity log (admins can see everyone's). This communicates with the server and is available in the account icon menu.
  • Contact Us: Users can send a message through a form in the sidebar, and I will receive it via email.
  • Carbon Footprint Calculator & Water Usage Calculator: Tools to help users understand their eco-friendliness. Accessible from the side menu.
  • About Us: Information about me and my partner. Accessible from the side menu.
  • Learn More: More information about climate change and the world's current problems. Accessible from the side menu or the home page.

What was hard to do?

The frontend was particularly challenging, especially as it was my first time using React and designing a proper website. I spent a lot of time on the design because it really bothered me when things didn't look right. Also, when we started the project, I was in Mexico, and my partner was in Israel. We decided to split the work, with me handling the frontend and him the backend. However, this created communication issues, and I didn’t understand big parts of his code, and he didn’t understand mine. After we realized this method wasn’t working, we switched to working together via screen sharing. Although the different time zones still made things difficult, this approach worked better.

Database Information

We are using SQLite as the database with Prisma ORM to make queries. The database file is named "dev.db." I recently realized it should be called "persist.js," but I left it as is to avoid losing data. I hope this isn’t a problem.
